Opiods

Opiods are drugs that relieve pain by interacting with receptors in the brain, including prescription medications like oxycodone and morphine, as well as illegal substances like heroin. Methadone, Suboxone, Sublocade, and Kadian (SROM), often combined to support recovery and safer use.

Concurrent Disorders

A concurrent disorder occurs when a person experiences both a substance use problem and a mental health issue, such as depression, anxiety, or PTSD. Screening for underlying mental health disorders helps provide integrated treatment, as outcomes often improve when one doctor addresses both issues together through counseling and/or medications.

Other Substance Use Disorders

Anti-craving medications are available for many substances including alcohol, benzodiazepines, cocaine/methamphetamine/stimulants, cannabis, tobacco, gambling disorder, and others. Counselling is offered in combination with medications.
