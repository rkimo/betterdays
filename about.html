About Better Days Clinic

Better Days Clinic welcomes anyone looking to stop or reduce substance use. We know it may be difficult to seek help, but whenever you’re ready, we’re here to support your recovery journey with compassion and respect.

We offer evidence-based, patient-centered care for all substances and any related mental health challenges. Effective treatment often combines counseling and medication, and many people do recover from substance use disorders.

Our focus is on in-person care to build stronger therapeutic connections, but telehealth options are also available in selective circumstances.

Most people self-refer. Call or email to book an assessment. We’re also happy to work with your family physician if you have one.



About Dr. Kimacovich

Dr. Kimacovich has practiced medicine since 1988. He is former Chief of Emergency at St. Catharines General and Hotel Dieu. During his 26-year emergency career, he developed a passion for the care of substance use and mental health patients who regularly presented to the emergency department.

“Dr. K” began substance care in 2005 at Out and About, Ontario’s first public opiate agonist clinic. He was appointed first Physician Lead of Addiction Medicine at Niagara Health where he managed substance use and concurrent mental health disorders with Psychiatry colleagues. At NH, he started one of Ontario’s first Inpatient Addictions Consultation Service.

In 2016, he created one of the first four pilot RAAM clinics in the world. RAAM (Rapid Access Addictions Medicine) pioneered low-barrier substance use care, medication-assisted treatment for substances, counseling, and treatment of concurrent mental health disorders. The RAAM model is now replicated around the world.

Dr. Kimacovich is certified in Family Practice, Emergency Medicine (FCFP EM), and Addictions (Fellow American Board of Addictions Medicine) and holds an appointment at McMaster University Michael DeGroote School of Medicine.
