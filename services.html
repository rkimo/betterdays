Treatment is available for many substances including opiates, alcohol, benzodiazepines, cocaine/methamphetamine/stimulants, cannabis, tobacco, gambling, and others.



Opiate Use Disorder

Opiate agonist treatment includes Methadone, Suboxone, Sublocade, Kadian (SROM), Safer Opiate Supply, and combinations of above.



Other Substance Use Disorders

Opiate agonist treatment includes Methadone, Suboxone, Sublocade, Kadian (SROM), Safer Opiate Supply, and combinations of above.



Concurrent Disorders

A concurrent disorder is when a substance problem and a mental health problem both occur in the same person at the same time. Self-medication of mental health problems with substances is common and can lead to problematic substance use (addiction).

All patients will be screened for underlying mental health disorders. If a diagnosis is made, treatment with counselling and/or medications will be offered. Most people have better outcomes when one doctor treats both the substance and the mental health problem together rather than multiple doctors with separate treatments.

Common co-occurring mental health problems may include depression, anxiety, bipolar, PTSD, ADHD, and psychosis.
